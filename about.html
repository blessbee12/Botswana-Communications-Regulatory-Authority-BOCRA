Who we are

An independent voice for Botswana's communications sector

The Botswana Communications Regulatory Authority (BOCRA) is an independent statutory body established under the BOCRA Act of 2012. We are responsible for regulating communications services — including telecommunications, broadcasting, postal services, and radio frequency spectrum — across the Republic of Botswana.

BOCRA succeeded the Botswana Telecommunications Authority (BTA), inheriting a legacy of sound regulation while expanding its mandate to meet the demands of a rapidly evolving digital landscape. We operate at arm's length from government and industry, ensuring our decisions are impartial, evidence-based, and in the public interest.

Our work touches every Motswana — from the mobile phone in your hand to the broadcaster on your television screen.

What we regulate

Our regulatory mandate

BOCRA's mandate is set out in the BOCRA Act and spans four principal areas of communications regulation in Botswana.

Telecommunications

BOCRA licenses and regulates all providers of electronic communications services, including mobile networks, fixed-line operators, internet service providers, and data networks.

  • Network service licensing & renewal
  • Quality of service monitoring
  • Interconnection & access regulation
  • Number portability oversight
  • Mobile money & fintech coordination

Broadcasting

BOCRA regulates all radio and television broadcasters — both public and private — to ensure diverse, fair, and quality content that serves the Botswana public.

  • Commercial and community broadcaster licensing
  • Content standards enforcement
  • Digital migration oversight (DVB-T2)
  • Must-carry and must-offer rules
  • Election broadcast impartiality

Spectrum management

BOCRA plans, allocates, assigns, and monitors the use of radio frequency spectrum — a scarce national resource that underpins all wireless communications.

  • National Frequency Allocation Plan
  • Spectrum licensing for commercial & private use
  • Type approval for radio equipment
  • Interference resolution & monitoring
  • 5G spectrum planning & assignment

Consumer protection

BOCRA serves as the primary consumer protection body for communications services — investigating complaints, setting service standards, and ensuring operators treat consumers fairly.

  • Complaint investigation & resolution
  • Consumer rights education
  • Service level agreement enforcement
  • Price regulation & tariff review
  • Vulnerable consumer protections
Our history

A journey of principled regulation

From the liberalisation of Botswana's telecom sector to the digital age — key milestones in BOCRA's story.

1996

Botswana Telecommunications Authority established

The BTA is formed as Botswana's first communications regulator, overseeing the nascent telecom sector and facilitating initial liberalisation.

2000

Mobile network licences awarded

BTA awards the first competitive mobile network licences, marking the beginning of mobile telecommunications competition in Botswana.

2008

Broadband infrastructure expansion begins

Regulatory frameworks for broadband internet services are introduced, spurring investment in national fibre and wireless broadband infrastructure.

2012

BOCRA established under the BOCRA Act

Parliament enacts the BOCRA Act, creating the Botswana Communications Regulatory Authority as the successor to BTA with an expanded mandate covering broadcasting and spectrum.

2017

Digital broadcasting migration launched

BOCRA oversees Botswana's transition from analogue to digital television broadcasting under the DVB-T2 standard, aligning with the ITU switchover mandate.

2021

National Digital Transformation Strategy alignment

BOCRA aligns its Strategic Plan with Botswana's National Digital Transformation Strategy, embedding digital inclusion, cybersecurity, and 5G readiness into its regulatory agenda.

2024

5G spectrum consultation and assignment process begins

BOCRA initiates the formal public consultation on 5G spectrum allocation, paving the way for next-generation mobile networks across urban and rural Botswana.

Legal framework

Legislation & instruments

BOCRA exercises its functions within a clearly defined legislative framework. The following Acts and regulations govern communications regulation in Botswana.

BOCRA Act 2012

The primary legislation establishing BOCRA's mandate, powers, governance, and functions.

Communications Regulatory Authority Act Cap 72:05

Consolidating legislation covering electronic communications services, licensing, and spectrum management.

Broadcasting Act Cap 72:04

Governs the licensing and regulation of radio and television broadcasting services in Botswana.

Consumer Protection Act 2018

Sets minimum consumer rights and protection standards applicable across all regulated services.

Cybercrime and Computer Related Crimes Act 2018

Provides BOCRA with an advisory role in matters of cyber regulation and electronic communications security.

Postal Services Act 2009

Governs the regulation of postal and courier services within BOCRA's expanded mandate.

International memberships

BOCRA is an active participant in regional and international regulatory bodies, contributing to global and African communications governance.

International Telecommunication Union (ITU)

UN specialised agency for information and communication technologies.

Communications Regulators Association of Southern Africa (CRASA)

Regional body coordinating communications regulation across SADC member states.

African Telecommunications Union (ATU)

Pan-African organisation promoting ICT development across the continent.

Commonwealth Telecommunications Organisation (CTO)

Facilitating digital development and policy exchange across Commonwealth nations.
