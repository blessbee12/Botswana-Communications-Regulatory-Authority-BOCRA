Know the Threats

Common Telecommunications Scams

Recognising a scam is the first step to protecting yourself. Below are the most frequently reported types of fraud affecting Botswana's communications consumers.

SIM-Swap Fraud

Criminals deceive mobile operators into transferring your phone number to a SIM card they control, gaining access to your accounts and banking.

  • Sudden loss of mobile signal
  • Unable to make calls or send messages
  • Unexpected banking OTPs not received
  • Unauthorised account access notifications

Impersonation Calls

Fraudsters pose as BOCRA officials, network operators, or government agencies to extract personal information or demand payments.

  • Caller claims to be from BOCRA or BTA
  • Requests for payment via mobile money
  • Threats of service disconnection
  • Urgency and pressure to act immediately

Smishing (SMS Scams)

Fraudulent text messages designed to steal your personal data, banking credentials, or trick you into calling premium-rate numbers.

  • Unexpected prize or lottery notifications
  • Links to suspicious websites
  • Requests for PIN or password via SMS
  • Messages from unknown shortcodes

Online & Data Fraud

Scams exploiting internet services, social media, and fraudulent websites to steal information or money from consumers.

  • Fake telecom operator websites
  • Data bundle resale scams
  • Wi-Fi hotspot credential theft
  • Social media impersonation of operators

Premium Rate Abuse

Unauthorised subscription services and premium-rate calls that generate charges on your bill without your knowledge or consent.

  • Unexplained charges on your mobile bill
  • Subscription services you didn't sign up for
  • Rapid data or airtime depletion
  • Premium SMS received unsolicited

Identity & Account Theft

Criminals using stolen personal information to fraudulently register SIM cards or open communications accounts in your name.

  • Unexpected new account notifications
  • Bills for services you never used
  • SIM cards registered under your ID
  • Calls from unknown numbers claiming to be you
Protect Yourself

What to Do — and What Not to Do

Follow these guidelines to keep your information and money safe.

Never Do This

  • Share your PIN, password, or OTP with anyone — including "officials"
  • Pay fees or fines via mobile money on instruction of a caller
  • Click links in unexpected SMS messages
  • Confirm personal details to unsolicited callers
  • Respond to messages promising prizes you didn't enter for
  • Allow others to register SIM cards in your name
  • Transfer money to "hold" a prize or reward

Always Do This

  • Verify caller identity by calling the official operator number
  • Enable two-factor authentication on all accounts
  • Regularly review your mobile billing statements
  • Report suspicious activity to BOCRA immediately
  • Contact your network operator if you lose signal unexpectedly
  • Keep your national identity documents secure
  • Educate family members, especially the elderly, about scams

Recent Scam Alerts

Fake BOCRA Officials Demanding Licence Renewal Fees via Mobile Money

Fraudsters are calling consumers posing as BOCRA inspectors and demanding immediate payment for "licence renewals" via Orange Money or MyZaka. BOCRA does not collect fees via mobile money. Do not pay and report the call immediately.

20 March 2026 Impersonation High Priority

Surge in SIM-Swap Attacks Targeting Mobile Banking Customers

BOCRA and mobile operators have recorded a significant increase in SIM-swap fraud targeting customers of Botswana's major banks. Consumers are urged to contact their bank and network operator immediately if they experience unexplained loss of mobile service.

12 March 2026 SIM-Swap

Warning: Smishing Campaign Targeting BTC Internet Service Customers

Customers of BTC's fibre broadband service are receiving fraudulent SMS messages claiming their account has been suspended and directing them to fake login pages designed to steal credentials. Do not click any links.

5 March 2026 Smishing
