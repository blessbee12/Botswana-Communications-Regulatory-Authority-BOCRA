Our Mandate

What BOCRA regulates

Established under the Botswana Communications Regulatory Authority Act, BOCRA oversees the full spectrum of communications and postal services in Botswana.

Telecommunications

Licensing and regulating voice, data, broadband, and internet service providers to ensure quality, fair pricing, and coverage across Botswana.

Learn more

Broadcasting

Regulating radio and television broadcasters including content standards, licensing of channels, and ensuring diverse, local programming for citizens.

Learn more

Postal services

Overseeing BotswanaPost and courier operators to maintain reliable, accessible, and affordable postal services for all communities.

Learn more

Spectrum management

Allocating and managing radio frequency spectrum for interference-free use, supporting 5G readiness, digital migration, and aviation communications.

Learn more

Consumer Protection

Know your rights as a consumer

BOCRA protects consumers from unfair practices by service providers. Here's how to file a complaint and what to expect.

1

Contact your service provider first

Before escalating to BOCRA, raise your concern directly with your service provider and allow them 7 working days to resolve it.

2

Submit complaint to BOCRA

If unresolved, submit a formal complaint via our online portal, phone (+267 392 3165), email, or in person at our Gaborone office.

3

Receive your reference number

You will receive a unique case reference number to track your complaint status at any time through our portal.

4

BOCRA investigates & resolves

Our consumer team investigates and aims to resolve complaints within 30 working days, keeping you informed throughout the process.
