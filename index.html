Our Mandate

Sectors we regulate

BOCRA oversees five communications sectors under the Communications Regulatory Authority Act 2012, protecting consumers and enabling competition.

Telecommunications

Licensing and oversight of mobile, fixed-line, and internet service providers across Botswana.

Learn more

Broadcasting

Regulation of commercial radio and television stations including Yarona FM, Duma FM, Gabz FM and eBotswana.

Learn more

Internet & ICTs

Facilitating growth of the internet market and overseeing ICT development in partnership with industry.

Learn more

Postal Services

Supervision of postal services to ensure safe, reliable, and affordable delivery for all Batswana.

Learn more

Consumer Protection

Know your rights as a consumer

BOCRA protects consumers from unfair practices by service providers. Here's how to file a complaint and what to expect.

1

Contact your service provider first

Before escalating to BOCRA, raise your concern directly with your service provider and allow them 7 working days to respond.

2

Submit complaint to BOCRA

If unresolved, submit a formal complaint via our online portal, phone (+267 392 3165), email, or visit our Gaborone office.

3

Receive your reference number

You'll receive a unique case reference number to track your complaint status at any time through our portal.

4

BOCRA investigates & resolves

Our consumer team investigates and aims to resolve complaints within 30 working days, keeping you informed throughout.
